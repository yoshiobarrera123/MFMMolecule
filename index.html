July 2024 C60

Fullerene C60, also known as buckminsterfullerene, is a spherical molecule composed of 60 carbon atoms arranged in a structure resembling a soccer ball. Discovered in 1985 by Harold Kroto, Richard Smalley, and Robert Curl, this unique carbon form earned them the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1996. C60 exhibits remarkable stability and electronic properties, valuable in materials science, electronics, and nanotechnology.
